Carbon Footprint

Let's learn about Carbon emissions cause at every stage of the Food Production Lifecycle

Land use Change and Farm

Across all foods, the land use and farm stages of the supply chain account for 80% of GHG emissions. In beef production, for example, there are three key contributing factors to the carbon footprint at these stages: animal feed, land conversion, and methane production from cows.

Animal Feed

The Greenhouse Gas emissions in the Animal Feed stage of the Food production cycle is caused as a result of crop production and its processing into feed for producing livestock

Food Processing

Carbon emissions in this stage results due to energy used in the process of converting raw agricultural products into final food items

Transport

Emissions from energy used in the transport of food items in the country as well as internationally. This stage of the supply chain makes up 10% of total GHG emissions on average.
